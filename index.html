Audio samples for "Attention-Guided Generative Adversarial Network for Whisper to Normal Speech Conversion (AGAN-W2SC) using Mel-Spectrogram"

Summary: Whispered speech is a special way of pronunciation, there is no vocal cord vibration when whispering. Therefore, a whispered speech does not contain a fundamental frequency, and its energy is about 20dB lower than that of its normal speech. Converting a whispered speech into a normal speech can improve speech quality and intelligibility. This demo shows the use of our attention-guided generative adversarial network for whisper to normal speech conversion, with which the mel-spectrogram of a converted normal speech is estimated at the frame level from the mel-spectrogram of its whispered speech, and the Griffin-Lim algorithm is then adopted to synthesize the converted normal speech mel-spectrogram into a time-domain waveform. The proposed AGAN-W2SC method avoids the difficulty of estimating the fundamental frequency of a normal speech from a whispered speech. This method can be adapted for practical applications because it does not require to align the associated speech parameters for training. Simulation results show that our GAN-W2SC method can obtain improved speech quality and speech intelligibility compared with those obtained by traditional methods based on dynamic time warping.
